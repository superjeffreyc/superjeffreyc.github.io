Pork Belly with Bean Curd

Ingredients

  • Pork belly (pork shoulder or pork butt will work as alternatives)
  • Dry Bean Curd
  • Scallion
  • Soy Sauce
  • Dark Soy Sauce
  • Chinese cooking wine
  • Brown sugar

Steps

  1. Blanch the pork belly (place in a pot of water, bring to boil, and turn off flame for 30 minutes)
  2. Pour out all the water and rinse the pork belly thoroughly to remove impurities
  3. Cut the pork belly into thin, bite sized slices (Note: it is still raw)
  4. Clean the pot and place the slices of pork belly back into the pot
  5. Add a few swirls of soy sauce, chinese cooking wine, and dark soy sauce to the pot (a swirl is one circular motion around the pot)
  6. Add some cold water to the pot to bring the total liquid volume to about half of the pork belly volume
  7. Add a small stick of brown sugar
  8. Keep on medium-low flame and let it simmer for several hours
  9. In the meantime, pour the dry bean curd into a bowl and submerge in cold water to soften it
  10. Chop the scallions
  11. Once the pork belly has reduced slightly, consider draining the oil and removing impurities to make it healthier and presentable
  12. Add more salt/sugar as needed
  13. Once the liquid has almost reduced, add in the dry bean curd and mix well
  14. The sauce should have a much thicker texture when its done
  15. Add the chopped scallions as a garnish
